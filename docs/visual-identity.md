# De Raaskalderij Visual Identity

## Positioning

De Raaskalderij should feel like a Flemish newspaper that has been taking itself seriously for far too long. The visual system should look credible in a feed for half a second, then reveal the absurdity through the headline.

Core tension: official, old-newspaper authority plus one small modern satirical sting.

## Logo

Primary profile mark: `assets/deraaskalderij-profile-pic.png`

Use the round `DR.` badge for Instagram, avatars, watermarks, and post corners. It is intentionally simple enough to read at profile-picture size. The orange period is part of the identity: it acts like a final editorial judgment.

Avoid using the full name inside the profile picture. At Instagram sizes, the monogram is stronger.

## Palette

- Ink black: `#111111`
- Paper: `#F5F0E6`
- Signal orange: `#F15A24`
- Flemish yellow: `#F2C94C`
- Newsprint grey: `#D8D1C4`
- Bureau blue: `#2F4858`

Use black and paper as the base. Orange is the recurring punchline marker: dots, labels, rules, quote marks, or one small icon. Yellow should be rare and institutional, not cheerful.

## Typography

Masthead and headline style:
- Bold serif, condensed if available.
- Suggested fonts: Georgia Bold, Libre Baskerville Bold, Playfair Display Black, or a similar newspaper serif.
- Headlines should feel written by a serious editor describing a ridiculous situation.

Supporting text:
- A neutral sans serif for labels, metadata, and small captions.
- Suggested fonts: Inter, Helvetica Neue, Arial.

## Instagram Post System

### 1. Headline Card

Best for fast feed jokes.

- Off-white paper background.
- Small top label: `BINNENLAND`, `POLITIEK`, `MEDIA`, `VLAANDEREN`, `BRUSSEL`, `SPORT`.
- Large black serif headline.
- Orange dot or underline near the end of the headline block.
- `DR.` badge in a corner.

### 2. Fake Photo News Card

Best for topics that need an image.

- Muted realistic image or public-domain-looking editorial scene.
- Black lower-third headline bar.
- Small orange section tag.
- Keep the joke in the headline, not in the image.

### 3. Quote Card

Best for politicians, institutions, and fake spokespeople.

- One large quote.
- Attribution underneath: name plus overly specific title.
- Orange quotation mark or full stop.

## Voice Rules

- Write like a sober Flemish news editor, not like a meme page.
- Let institutions speak in deadpan bureaucratic language.
- Prefer specific Flemish references: VRT, NMBS, De Lijn, Agentschap Wegen en Verkeer, gemeenteraad, onderwijskoepels, subsidies, overlegcomites, lintbebouwing.
- The first line should be a complete joke.
- Captions may add one extra twist, but should not explain the joke.

## Example Format

Headline:

> Vlaamse overheid lanceert wachtlijst voor mensen die op wachtlijst willen

Caption:

> BRUSSEL - Om de druk op bestaande wachtlijsten te verlichten, start Vlaanderen met een voorbereidende wachtlijst voor burgers die zich binnenkort op een echte wachtlijst willen plaatsen.
>
> "Zo creeren we eindelijk perspectief voor mensen die nog nergens op wachten," klinkt het bij de administratie.

## Do Not

- Do not imitate De Speld's exact layout, masthead, or brand system.
- Do not make it look too glossy, corporate, or meme-like.
- Do not overuse orange.
- Do not put long paragraphs in images.
- Do not rely on dialect spelling for humor. Keep the Flemishness in the references and rhythm.
